Where hope and daylight dies

发表于2022-08-13|更新于2022-08-13|音乐
|阅读量:

Where hope and daylight dies

原歌词：

Still here I wake and I think of you
I see you far away
Answer my call
Can you hear my voice
I hear you

For we are gone and forever lost
Broken here I lie
Beneath the shadow sink
Where daylight dies
I wake for you

In better lands the sun may shine
And green leaves on trees spring
Their opening and blossoming
But here the raven sing

But still I stand and think of
Days when grass was green
And my heart was so young
They ‘ve never been past
Forever lost

整首歌词改编自J.R.R Tolkien的诗I sit upon the stones alone。以下为原文：

I sit upon the stones alone;
the fire is burning red,
the tower is tall, the mountains dark;
all living things are dead.
In western lands the sun may shine,
there flower and the tree in spring
is opening, is blossoming;
and there the finches sing.
But here I sit alone and think
of days when grass was green,
and earth was brown, and I was young:
they might have never been.
For they are past, for ever lost,
and here the shadows lie
deep upon my heavy heart,
and hope and daylight die.
But still I sit and think of you;
I see you far away
Walking down the homely roads
on a bright and windy day.
It was merry then when I could run
to answer to your call,
could hear your voice or take your hand;
but now the night must fall.
And now beyond the worldI sit;
and know not where you lie!
O master dear, will you not hear
my voice before we die?

在《魔戒》的手稿中，这首诗歌出现在卷六的第一章《奇立斯乌苟之塔》中。第三纪元3019年3月13日，弗罗多被蜘蛛希洛布蛰晕后，被奥克带进奇立斯乌苟之塔，山姆为了解救自己的好友，只身一人闯进奇立斯乌苟之塔的大门。在塔内四处搜寻的山姆最终精疲力竭，绝望中他在冰冷黑暗的塔楼里唱起了这首I sit upon the stones alone。

不过在《魔戒》之后的正式版本里这首诗被更换成了In western lands，也许是托尔金觉得原版显得山姆过于绝望，而正式版中的In western lands可能更符合一个疲惫的霍比特人想摆脱眼前的场景而随机在记忆中抓取美好的片段，在对往日的回忆中逐渐唤回勇气继续走下去。里面提到的精灵之星和山姆之后在魔多瞥见的那颗星应该是同一个，都是指埃雅仁迪尔之星。这里也顺手放上In western lands原文以及邓版翻译对照：

In western lands beneath the Sun
西部国度里，阳光下，
the flowers may rise in Spring,
在春天，也许有繁花生长，
the trees may bud, the waters run,
也许树梢萌芽，活水流淌，
the merry finches sing.
还有鸣雀欢快歌唱。
Or there maybe ‘tis cloudless night
或者还有晴朗无云的夜晚，
and swaying beeches bear
摇曳的山毛榉，纷披发叶
the Elven-stars as jewels white
戴着精灵之星，
amid their branching hair.
犹如宝石白亮。

Though here at journey’s end I lie
虽然我倒卧在，长途跋涉的终点，
in darkness buried deep,
黑暗把我深深埋葬，可是
beyond all towers strong and high,
越过所有坚墙高塔，
beyond all mountains steep,
越过所有险峻大山，
above all shadows rides the Sun
高挂在所有阴影之上，太阳运行不息，群星永在：
I will not say the Day is done,
我绝不认为时日已尽，
nor bid the Stars farewell.
也不打算向群星永别。
